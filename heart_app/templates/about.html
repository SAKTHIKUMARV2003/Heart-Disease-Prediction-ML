Understanding Heart Disease

Heart disease encompasses a range of conditions that affect the heart and blood vessels, including coronary artery disease, arrhythmias, and heart failure. These conditions are influenced by factors such as genetics, lifestyle, and environmental exposure.

Key risk factors include:

  • High blood pressure
  • High cholesterol
  • Smoking and alcohol consumption
  • Diabetes and obesity
  • Stress and physical inactivity

Prevention strategies like regular exercise, balanced nutrition, and routine health checks are essential to maintaining heart health.

What Happens During a Heart Health Checkup?

During a session like this, doctors typically check for:

  • Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)
  • Coronary Artery Disease
  • Irregular Heartbeat (Arrhythmia)
  • Heart Valve Problems
  • Heart Failure Symptoms
  • Atherosclerosis (Arterial Blockage)

This makes the session valuable not only for patients with known heart disease but also for those at risk due to age, lifestyle, or genetics.
